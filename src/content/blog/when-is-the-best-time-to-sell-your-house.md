### Best Time to Sell Your House

Historically, the month of May has been the best time of year to sell a house, signaling the transition between spring and summer. However, in recent years, that has moved up a couple of months to March and April.

This lines up with the local market trend, as spring is usually when many homes get put on the real estate market. Spring is usually considered optimal as it follows a time when people have been cooped up in their homes during the cold winter months. Spring and summer also tend to be the easiest seasons to actually show your house. The bright, warm weather makes it easy to market with curb appeal, and more people are willing to actually go to house showings or an open house event when the weather is nice.

You generally want to find the right buyer before September. This is when families with kids settle down for the school year. By October, profits tend to be at their lowest, which may impact the market value of your home.

### Local Markets

At the same time, it’s important to understand that every local market is different. The general rule of thumb: the best time of year to sell a house is also the best time of year to enjoy it.

For example, many people usually go to the south for the winter, which is why November and December are ideal for selling a home in Florida. Beach houses in the area tend to reach their peak season right before the summer months, when people are about to enjoy their summer vacation, or immediately after the summer months as people return home from vacation. Similarly, ski towns and winter cabins tend to see their peak during the winter season as an influx of visitors are eager to enjoy the snow and slopes.

### Selling Out of Season

Finding a prospective buyer when selling out of season can seem like a challenge, but it can potentially come with its perks. For example, spring is an ideal time to sell, but that also means everyone is putting their house up for sale, resulting in a higher degree of competition. You have to make yourself stand out in order to secure that home sale, which means doing extra work to prep your property and having a much deeper understanding of the local real estate markets. 

Furthermore, selling in the fall might not be great for families with school-aged kids, but it can be a good time for any other interested buyer who is also house hunting. Fall tends to be when shoppers are moving because of job relocation, layoffs, and family issues so beware of this trend when assessing buyer demand.

If you’re not sure exactly when to sell your house and you have a flexible timeline, shoot for spring or early summer. That said, selling a house is not an exact science. People sell their homes all year long, whether the real estate market is at its peak, plateau, or valley. As long as your local market conditions generate some buyer demand, you always have a chance of selling at a profit. All it can take is one very interested buyer  – maybe even an all-cash buyer – who can meet if not exceed your asking price and agree to your closing conditions.
