### Median Home Price San Bernardino County

As of February 2022, the median home price in San Bernardino county was $475,000. Additionally, the median _sold_ home price was $465,000, rendering it a slight seller’s market.1

With that said, the city of San Bernardino’s housing market differs from the county’s. With a median home listing price of $449,900, homes in the city of San Bernardino are more affordable than the county’s average.2

Of course, the city’s median home price doesn’t tell the _whole_ story. 

For a more accurate breakdown of the median home price in San Bernardino, CA, it’s important to look at [San Bernardino neighborhoods](https://flipsplit.com/blog/san-bernardino-county-median-home-price-by-neighborhood)’ home values.

### Ridgeline: The Most Expensive 

Located just north of downtown San Bernardino, the city’s Ridgeline neighborhood is known for its laidback suburban living. Given its respectable schools and family-friendly amenities, the neighborhood consists of families and young professionals.

## Housing Market

At $599,000, the median home listing price for the Ridgeline neighborhood is higher than the city’s average. If you’re thinking of moving to the neighborhood or selling your Ridgeline home, it’s paramount to consider the following:3

-   The median home listing price per square foot is $281
    
-   As of January 2022, homes in Ridgeline were on the market for an average of 50 days
    
-   The neighborhood is a balanced market
    

While the neighborhood’s median home price nears $600,000, it’s possible to find homes for as little as $250,000 and as high as $679,000.3

### University Square: Best for Students and Young Professionals

Encompassing the Cal State San Bernardino campus, the University Square neighborhood is loaded with delicious restaurants, hip art galleries, and student-friendly living.

What’s more, the neighborhood’s proximity to downtown San Bernardino makes it a popular neighborhood for homebuyers desiring a “city feel” minus the “city traffic.”

![](https://flipsplit.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/What-Is-The-San-Bernardino-Median-Home-Price1.jpg)

## Housing Market

University Square’s median home listing price of $449,900 matches the city’s average. To that end, here are a few other housing market metrics to consider:4

-   The median listing price per square foot is $341
    
-   The median sold home price is $335,000
    
-   On average, homes in University Square are on the market for 69 days
    
-   The neighborhood is a slight seller’s market
    

If you’re selling your University Square home, you should inform prospective homebuyers of the notable schools and exciting nightlife. It’s not just the home that’s going to influence the sale price; the accessibility of the location is also paramount. 

### La Plaza: Most Affordable

Located just west of downtown San Bernardino, the La Plaza neighborhood features the city’s most affordable housing. In addition, the neighborhood is known for its many parks, most notably Encanto Park. As of 2020, the neighborhood had a population of 5,321.5

## Housing Market

At $325,000, La Plaza’s median listing price is lower than both the city’s and county’s average. Sellers and buyers should also note the following:6

-   The median listing price per square foot is $309
    
-   The median sold home price is $345,000
    
-   On average, homes are on the market for 49 days
    
-   The neighborhood is a seller’s market
    

Although La Plaza is the city’s most affordable neighborhood, it also has the widest range of housing prices. La Plaza’s home prices vary, so whether you are looking for a single family home or a quaint apartment, it’s possible to find a home in the neighborhood for as little as $114,000 and as high as $2,100,000. La Plaza’s housing market tends to evoke variability. New home buyers should look here as there is a variety of options available.

### Verdemont: Most Seller-Friendly

Nestled in the city’s northernmost limit, the Verdemont neighborhood is San Bernardino’s most seller-friendly neighborhood. This is because the neighborhood features well-rated schools, suburban living, and access to outdoor activities.

## Housing Market

The median listing home price for the Verdemont neighborhood is $547,500, making it higher than the city’s and county’s average. If you’re a prospective Verdemont home buyer or seller, it’s imperative that you know the following:7

-   The median price per square foot is $331
    
-   The median sold home price is $580,000
    
-   Homes in Verdemont are on the market for an average of 39 days
    
-   Verdemont is a strong seller’s market
